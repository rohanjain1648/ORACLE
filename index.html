01 — The Problem

Prediction markets are systematically mispriced

📰 Stale Information

Human traders rely on training-data knowledge and manual research. Breaking news takes hours to propagate into prices.

⚓ Anchoring Bias

Prices cluster near round numbers — 50%, 70%, 90% — because traders anchor on salient reference points rather than base rates.

🎲 Uncalibrated Confidence

Most participants trade on gut feel. Studies show individuals are systematically overconfident on hard questions and underconfident on easy ones.

💸 Poor Position Sizing

Fixed-size trading ignores conviction. Traders bet the same on a 10% edge as on a 30% edge, destroying expected value.

The core question: Can an AI agent exploit these inefficiencies systematically, at scale,
without human intervention — and make a profit doing it?

03 — Innovation

Four ideas. None combined before.

  • 1
    Superforecaster Prompt Engineering
    Five-step structured decomposition (reference class → inside view → outside view → market anchor → calibration check) borrowed from Tetlock's Good Judgment Project. Forces explicit reasoning instead of pattern-matching to a number.
  • 2
    Extended Thinking for Hard Markets
    Claude Sonnet 4.6's native extended thinking allocates 5,000 dedicated reasoning tokens before producing a final probability — "slow thinking" for complex, multi-factor events where the answer isn't obvious.
  • 3
    Fractional Kelly Criterion Sizing
    Mathematically optimal position sizing: bet proportional to conviction. 25% fractional Kelly maximizes expected log-wealth with a 4× safety margin for imperfect probability estimates.
  • 4
    Live Web Evidence Per Tick
    Two targeted Tavily searches per market per tick surface breaking news, polls, and expert commentary — then synthesized by Claude before forecasting. Prices update every 15 minutes; ORACLE's evidence does too.

Why Extended Thinking?

Prediction markets require weighing multiple competing hypotheses. Extended thinking is "System 2" reasoning — deliberate, step-by-step — vs. the pattern-matching of standard generation. Empirically reduces overconfidence on hard questions.

07 — Position Sizing

The Kelly Criterion — mathematically optimal

For a binary market where YES costs q per share, our probability is p, and we have B dollars of available cash:

edge = |poracle − market_mid|
kelly_fraction = edge ÷ (1 − entry_price)
notional = kelly_fraction × 0.25 × available_cash

Market mid: 0.40 · Our p: 0.60
Edge: 0.20 · Kelly: 0.20/0.60 = 33%
Notional: 33% × 25% × $800 = $66 → capped $50

10 — Risk Management

Capital preservation first

🛑 Drawdown Halt

If portfolio loses more than 30% from peak equity, all trading stops for that tick. Prevents loss spirals.

SafeHalt at 30%

💰 Cash Reserve

Always keeps 20% of equity as uninvested cash. Ensures liquidity for high-conviction opportunities and covers fees.

20% reserve80% deployable

📊 Position Limits

Maximum 15 open positions at any time. Maximum 3 positions in any single topic family. Prevents over-concentration.

⚡ Minimum Edge Gate

Only trades when |p − market_mid| ≥ 5%. Sub-threshold markets are silently skipped — no noise trading that bleeds fees.

Philosophy: An agent that survives all 96 ticks with capital intact beats one that wins 3 big bets and blows up on the 4th. ORACLE is designed for the long game.

13 — Why This Will Win

Five unfair advantages

📐

Mathematically Optimal Sizing

Kelly Criterion is provably optimal for maximizing long-run wealth. Over 96 ticks this compounds — agents using fixed sizes leave expected value on the table.

📡

Evidence That Actually Updates

Markets are often priced on stale info. When ORACLE finds breaking news that shifts the true probability 10% and the market hasn't moved, that's a real, exploitable edge.

🧠

Structured Reasoning Cuts Hallucination

Open-ended prompts ("what's the probability?") let LLMs pattern-match to plausible numbers. Five-step decomposition forces explicit reasoning — empirically more calibrated.

🔮

Extended Thinking on Hard Questions

Claude's dedicated reasoning budget gives ORACLE a qualitative edge on complex multi-factor markets where surface features don't reveal the answer.

⑤ Capital preservation beats home runs. Agents that make 3 aggressive bets and blow up lose to agents with consistent positive expected value. ORACLE's risk management is built to stay in the game.
